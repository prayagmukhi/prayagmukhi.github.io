Introduction

Anxiety - a feeling of worry, nervousness, or unease about something with an uncertain outcome.

 

Anxiety is not a problem; it is the outcome of a problem.

There is no denying that being a 21-year-old is so much different today than it was just a decade ago. Now we have the worlds complete information at our fingertips. We are more self-aware of our situations than we have ever been before, because of the internet.

It is that vast knowledge that leads us to seriously question ourselves where we stand in this competitive world.

I was always worried that my friends would laugh at me when I tell them that I was planning to write this book, and would make fun of me writing about something that no one cares about, but to my surprise they were pretty supportive and wanted to share their personal experiences and anxieties, so that I can bring this situation into light.

In addition, many internet forums are filled with millennials who have their own problems with their personal anxieties and are just looking for someone to help them over the web.

However, the solution to anxiety is not outside, it is inside our own heads, to cure it, we will have to find the problem that has caused it, the reason why it affects us and then attacking it at its source by actually figuring out our own phycology.

The purpose of this book is to discuss those problems that have led many people in their early 20s towards anxiety, the reasons why it affects them and some possible solutions for it.

 

The Problem

Anxiety affects everyone differently, a problem for someone might be a blessing for another.

A large amount of people doesnt even feel anxiety in their early 20s even when they know that they have to take many major decisions that have the capacity to change the shape of their career and even life.

That happens not because they figured out their life; it is because they do not overthink about every step that they will have to take in their upcoming years of life. They feel ready for it and believe that they will handle any situation that comes in front of them, whenever it appears.

This mindset can be beneficial in some ways because a worry-free mind can think clearly and does not usually get scared by the upcoming obstacles of life.

However, sometimes it also leads to ignorance and lack of planning for the future, which in turn leads to a mediocre career or life when it could have been a great one.

For the majority of us that do feel anxious about the upcoming future, it is perfectly normal, almost everyone goes through this period in their lives, it may be different than it was a decade ago but it is not necessarily more difficult.

These are some of the major problems that can cause a 21-year-old anxiety.


 

End of College days

Fear is just our minds creation.

 

Over the course of our lives, we have heard time after time that the college days are the best years of our lives. We have heard that since we were in high school, from almost everyone older than us.

This makes us hyped in high school, and we start looking forward to having the best days of our lives after we get admission in a college. And most of us know pretty much instantly how overhyped this phenomenon is when we get an admission into a college.

 

In India, some of us graduate from a course with a 3-year curriculum and some of us with a 4-year curriculum, so for some, the anxiety hits when they are 21 and for some when they are 22 but hits us all the same. And it almost always follows this pattern.

 

1 or 2nd year We are hyped because we believe that these are going to be the best years of our lives because we are finally on our own for the first time in our lives and there is no sense of responsibility, and neither is there any anticipation of the future.

We make new friends, and this is the time that we have the most have fun and have the time of our lives.

 

2nd last year We Realize that this cannot go on forever and this fun attitude will probably not help us in shaping the career that is yet to come.

So, some of us try our hands at internships and personal projects to make us feel that we have at least started building our resume and we are doing something that can be written on it. This leads to a sense of accomplishment that makes us believe we not totally worthless, even if those internships and projects were worthless.

 

Final Year We realize that the end of this overhyped college life is right around the corner and we have done next to nothing that would help us prepare for the real life, which seems is going to start tomorrow. Weve always been told,college days are the best years of your lives. Now we start to wonder if it is all downhill from here, the fear sets in and that is when the anxiety kicks us.

 

The Competition

The race is not worth running if it is unwinnable.

 

According to Census 2011, Indias population is approximately 1.3 Billion (130 crores), and 9.2% of them are in the age group of 20 24.

This means that around 111 Million (1.1 crores) people are competing for almost the same number of jobs that were available a generation ago when the population was significantly lower.

 

The job market is crowded, and in the broken education system of India, students are not even taught the actual skills that are needed to perform the job that they are supposedly studying for.

 

This makes it even harder for employers to hire from a large number of unemployable graduates. Even if they decide to hire them, most companies have to train the graduates at their own cost so that they can attain the skills necessary to pull off the job.

 

Not just the job market, the competition for post-graduation courses like the MBAs have also never been higher before, and not just in Indian colleges but also in foreign universities of countries like USA, UK, Canada and Australia.

 

This happens because large amounts of people are doing the same thing that has been taught to them in masses.

 

Most of us are never sure what to do after high school, so we just take the advice from someone who we trust like our parents or teachers, and follow the path that they lead us to by believing that we will figure out what to do next ourselves.

For example, almost every commerce student in India is told that a Charted Accountant is highest paying job that a commerce student can get. That is why the majority of the people try their hand at it by taking its entrance exams without even giving a thought whether they like it or not.

Due to the difficulty of these entrance exams, only around 1% of the students that try are able to clear their exams and are able to become a Chartered accountant.

 

However, that is not the case in something like B.tech or BBA, which are just a curriculum that can be taught by any institution, and anyone can easily get an admission in almost any college that offers this course.

 

Due to this, many students have serious realization mid-curriculum that this is not the thing that they want to do anymore. This sometimes leads to existential crises and intense levels of anxiety. And those who are not lucky enough to realize that in their college are the ones who are the victims of quarter life crises.

 

Some students pursue multiple degrees, various online courses and everything they can, not just, because they can, but because they have to, in order to get ahead in this intense competition.

 

Also, the number of students that are starting their own businesses in college have also never been higher before, which only proves how desperate students are to break the traditional path of getting a job or doing a post-graduation after college just so they can get out of this fierce competition.

 

This intense competition in almost every field leads many 21 year olds to really question themselves whether they are actually doing what they want, or just following the crowd wherever it leads.

 

Finding Your Passion

We have to carve the things we cant find.

 

This is fairly a new concept that has recently spread across the globe due to the vast reach of the internet.

 

Many highly successful people such as Bill Gates, Steve Jobs, Warren Buffet and many others have advised youngsters to follow their passion if they want to become successful. The logic behind this is if you are able to find what you love then the work wouldnt be stressful and you would be the hardest working person in that field because you truly love what you do.

 

The advice seems simple enough, but the fact is that most of us dont know what our passion is. Some of us dont even know what a passion actually means, let alone finding it.

This sudden sense of finding our passion does feel like something worthwhile, but sometimes it is like trying to find something we dont even know exists.

Never the less, we try our hand in finding the thing that we were meant for. In that process, we discover even deeper concepts like finding your purpose in life, finding what you are best at and of course finding what kind of work you truly love.

This can even cause the paradox of choice, where we start evaluating so many choices that we end up taking no decision at all.

 

Just a decade ago, 21-year-olds were not even aware of these concepts, they were free as a bird, stress-less and gliding through life without giving it a second thought. That was because at that time work was perceived as just a thing that lets you earn your living.

But this is not the case today, now we are aware of these concepts, and they are tempting to be followed, now work does not represent as something you do just to earn a few bucks, but it is thing that is supposed to bring fulfillment and meaning to your life.

 

A few of us are lucky enough to find it early on in their 20s and are able to devote their rest of the lives towards it.

However, most of us do not find it that quickly, because how can someone find the work that they love if they have never worked anywhere before.

The truth is that some of us have more than one passion, and we cant just choose one. It is like choosing your favorite parent.

Some of us just have to work hard enough to convert our interest into our passion.

And some of us are just supposed to take multiple career pivots in life because we were never meant to find that one thing that we love to do above all else, which is also perfectly normal.

But, this overwhelming sense of finding your so called passion at such a young age can lead to anxiety for a lot of youngsters.

Sometimes even finding it can lead to anxiety when you realize that youre not good at the thing you love.

Relationships

The only people who have the capacity to affect our lives are the ones closest to us.

 

In India, some of us live with our parents, and some of us travel to another city for our college and aspirations, but almost everyone is financially dependent on their parents before graduating from college.

 

This is due to the close relationships that we have with our parents and that they are ready to support us on every step of the way for our future and are willing to help us in any way possible, financially, psychologically and sometimes even spiritually.

 

With this love and support also comes expectations. And sometimes with those expectations comes pressure. It is perfectly fair for parents to have expectations from their child but when those expectations makes them to pressure their own opinions and advice on the child, it becomes a burden.

 

This is a major problem that I have witnessed first-hand through my friends, and it is probably the most talked about topic in Indian societies.

 

Millennials definitely need guidance from their parents so they can achieve what they want, not what their parents want them to achieve.

 

This lack of understanding in Indian culture is due to the biggest generation gap between parents and this generations children.

This causes the lack of empathy that parents have for their children because they fail to relate to the problems of their children because they never faced them when they were at their childrens age.

Relationship problems are one of those things that Indian parents can never relate to.

Also, an astonishing number of children never tell their parents that they are in a relationship just so they do not get judged by their own parents like they are from the rest of the society.

 

This causes major problems, because when a person in their early 20s is not able to take help from their parents about a thing that they are clearly more experienced in, it leads to major relationship crises and sometimes even massive heartbreaks. It also increases the already existing anxiety of the person who now also has to deal with this situation all by themselves without the help of their parents.

 

On the other hand, India has one of the highest young population in the world, and a huge percentage of them are single. They have their own anxieties of whether they are going to ever find someone or not, which is pretty normal, but it certainly helps when you are able to seek advice from someone who has been through such situations like their parents.

Social Media

The negative is much easier to catch than the positive.

 

Social Media is probably the most powerful tool of this generation. We can do so much with its aid; we can connect to people we admire even if they are living an ocean away and learn from them, seek their advice, consult our mentors and other countless things that would just have been impossible just a decade ago.

Even the CEO of the billion-dollar company, Uber, Travis Kalanick met his first employee Ryan Graves using the social media platform Twitter.

There is no denying it is one of the greatest assets of our generation if used right but sometimes it also becomes that part of the problem that causes anxiety if it isnt used properly.

 

Everyone you have ever known is connected to you on these platforms, and they are constantly updating their statuses and sharing new photos on it, this can be a little too overwhelming for your eyes to see every time you open any of these social media apps.

This leads to the fear of missing out.

It is a popular phenomenon, which is happening in our generation. It means that something is happening out there that you are not part of and everyone in your social circle is aware of it and you dont want to be the only one who isnt.

That is why we keep coming back to these platforms to keep ourselves updated on news, trends, and gossips that makes us feel like we are part of a society.

 

Since we are constantly updated on your friends life, it leads us to feel envious of their achievements and sometimes we ourself use these platforms to show off our own achievements.

All of this is pretty normal in todays world, but this immense influence of our friends on our life was not normal previously.

This leads us to focus on the short-term goals and achievements that can be a showcase for our friends, instead of planning and working for our future and career which a 21-year-old is supposed to do.

 

Since we are only seeing the successes of your friends it leads us to question our own lives and why arent as successful as our friends, and this sometimes can even lead you to imitate what our friends are doing in life.

This is common behavioral pattern that I have seen loads of my friend slip into.

They see one of their friends having success in his career, and they get tempted to copy what that person is doing instead of doing the thing they were interested in. This makes us do things that neither we are interested in, nor we are good at, just in the hope of achieving the same success that our peers have achieved, instead of finding our own path to success.

 

This ever-refreshing feed on these social media platforms creates a bubble for us that only shows the things that we want to see and eats up most of the time in one of the most crucial periods of our lives and keeps us coming back for more.

 

The Reason

Not everyone is anxious about their future, and for some of us who are, the reason can be different from one another.

There are problems in everyones lives, but it affects everyone differently.

Same situation with different people can have polar opposite reactions to different people, like a break up can be depressing for someone and blissful for another, depending on their situations.

That is because every persons ideologies and their way of thinking are different.

But, even though there are so many different variables, there is still a recognizable pattern of reasons that can be observed in our generation that really causes us anxiety.

I have seen many friends and acquaintances who are the victims of such anxiety, and for almost all them, the reason was always one of these.

 

Influence

We are what we consume.

 

Influence of Content

 

In this day and age, we have countless channels on the television, a huge number of books, thousands of movies and so many social networks filled with so much content that we dont even have the capability to process. Yet we unknowingly consume it, consciously and unconsciously.

While we are scrolling through our feeds, we see countless images, countless articles for which we dont even stop scrolling, but some of that information is processed by our eyes and sent to our brain.

When we see a quote on social media that says Never quit your dream, by Steve Jobs or Jay Cutler or any other famous celebrity, even though we know that quote might not be authentic, it most certainly triggers the motivational sensor in our brains and definitely tends to inspire us in ways that nothing else could have done in that moment.

However, reading about a simple quote on the internet, this doesnt seem so harmful, after all, it ended up inspiring us, which cannot be bad any way imaginable, can it?

What would have happened if Drew Houston, the founder of the multi-billion-dollar organization name Dropbox, was also inspired by this quote and decided to live by it just because a famous, successful person said it?

He would have never quit his first startup, which was failing miserably and would have never moved on to work on his second startup Dropbox, which made him a billionaire.

 

Influence of people

 

Leaving behind the random posts on Social Media, the biggest influence on us is by the people that are closest to us, it can be our friends, our partner, our teachers or our parents.

If Brian Chesky had quit working on his little startup named Airbnb, even when all his friends had suggested him to quit and end his misery he would not have been the man that he is today.

He and his co-founder were rejected by a ton of Venture Capitalits in Silicon Valley, even by the most renowned ones like Chris Sacca. They were even told by many successful people in Silicon Valley that their idea was stupid. They had so much invested in their startup that they didnt even have the money to buy lunch let alone a place to live in.

But the right choice for them was to not quit and keep working on their dream while neglecting the advice of their friends and other successful people in Silicon Valley.

The point is that there is no one way that we can all just follow and become successful in our lives.

There can be a million ways to achieve the thing we want, but not all of them are going to work for us, we have to find the one that is going to work for us, ourselves.

Everyone has opinions, and some opinions have a bigger impact on us than the others, but the right decision should be made by us and not be the people who have the biggest influence on us.

 

Vastness of the amount of available Content

 

For all the content that we consume, some of it is chosen by us, and some of it is chosen for us, by the algorithms of search engines and social networks.

And all that content gives us knowledge, which we often use to make major decisions in life.

Lets say, two 21-year-olds have an idea, and they both want to start a startup, but arent sure if they should start up right away in college or wait and have few years of experience first.

So, both of them try to learn from more experienced and successful people who have made this decision in their lives.

One of them comes across a keynote of Paul Graham, a partner in the worlds biggest startup accelerator named Y Combinator. Paul Graham has strongly suggested youngsters not to start a startup in college because you are inexperienced, have no industry knowledge, no real connections, and possibly not enough skills to pull off the hard job of building a successful startup.

The other one came across an interview with Justin Kan, who is also a partner in the worlds biggest startup accelerator named Y Combinator. Justin says that the best time to start a startup is in college because you have unlimited time, you have access to multiple potential co-founders, and you have no other job so you have nothing to lose.

Both Paul Graham and Justin Kan are right in their own way and are only giving the advice that they themselves used that made them successful.

 

But these contracting advises only made those 21-year-olds more confused rather than helping them to make a firm decision.

 

Even when it comes to life advice, many successful people have given advises that are the polar opposite of each other.

For example, Gary Vaynerchuk, CEO of a 150-million-dollar revenue business and has one of the biggest personal brand in business says that hard work is the only controllable pillar of success and you should always put more time and effort at work if you want to become more successful, in fact, he himself is one of the hardest working people Ive seen and lives by his advice.

On the other hand, there is Alexis Ohanian, co-founder of Reddit, which is USs 8th most popular website in USA and also one of the biggest social networking sites, suggests that hard work is only going to decrease your productivity and make you hate your work more, who is also correct when it comes to his situation because he had sold his company early on.

Ultimately, whom should we listen to and follow?

Similarly, sometimes the same thing can teach a different lesson to different people because everyone has their own perception.

For example, a failed startup can lead someone to believe that it is hard and cannot be done, and the same thing can make another person more confident in their ability build the next Facebook.

These truckloads of content only confuses the already conflicted mind of a 21-year-old.

There will always be someone telling you to go one way or the other, but the only right path is the one that you create for yourself.

A same thing can be achieved through more than one way.

For example, a person who has determined to clean the holy waters of the River Ganga can choose to run for the governor so he can get it done, or he can start up a non-profit organization and start raising funds for it.

Both of them can work, but it is up to the person who has to decide, which one he can pull off.

 

The Picture

 

It would be safe to say that the average 21-year-old has little to no experience in the real world where you are not judged solely by your ability to cram information and dumping it on the answer sheet.

All we have is a picture of how life is going to be after college, in our minds, and that picture is painted by reading and listening to others experiences and their stories.

And with the use of the Internet, we can make try to make that picture clearer by soaking up every piece of information that is available out there.

And the more we try to make the picture clearer, the more we tend to get influenced by others thoughts, opinions, analogies, advises, lessons and beliefs.

We listen to so many stories and experiences that we even forget which of our thoughts were original and which of them were influenced by others.

Even while writing this book I realized how many of the thoughts that I am writing about were quite the opposite of my original beliefs before someone more experienced made me admit that I was wrong.

Sometimes, this unoriginal picture that we have painted of the real life for ourselves is perfectly accurate, sometimes it is completely nonsensical, sometimes it is positive and sometimes negative.

But, all it is, is an anticipation of the real life, not the real life itself, and the only thing that it causes is anxiety.

But, with so much information and knowledge out there, we cant help ourselves but paint that picture even though It doesnt do us any good.

Sometimes we just forget, life is not what you read, it is what you experience.

 

Illusion of Early Success

 

In this dynamic age of the internet, we read about all kinds of successful people, and in almost all sorts of fields.

Also, there are many prodigies (a young person with exceptional qualities or abilities) out there, who have been extremely successful very early on in their lives.

In fact, there is a culture of glorifying these youngsters in our society, that is why we see countless interviews and articles about these remarkable young people who have achieved the things so early on in their lives that so few in this world ever do.

Whether it be Justin Bieber, who became one of the most popular singers in the world before even he turned 20, or people like Mark Zuckerberg and Evan Spiegel have both been youngest billionaires in the world in their own times, or someone like Tatum O'Neal who won an Oscar at the age of 10, they all are covered in the news and media extensively.

What we dont read about are the millions of struggling teenage musicians and actors, or about thousands of startups that were started by young people and failed, every year.

This causes an average person in their early 20s to really question their skills and abilities required to achieve their goal. These glorified success stories make us believe that if were good enough, we would also have achieved our goals by now.

For some, this culture of celebrating only success, inspires some people, creates the thoughts of self-doubt for some and causes anxiety for others.

 

Lack of Patience

Rome Wasnt Built in one day, and neither was Facebook.

 

It has never been easier to start working towards our dreams and achieving our goals than today. With the use of the internet we can start learning almost anything including music, acting, directing, marketing, programming, business development and even things like law and medicine.

But, the thing is that it takes time and practice to become a world-class musician, an Oscar-winning actor or director, a capable programmer or a good businessman.

People see successful like Beyonc or Ronaldo today, what they dont see how much time they put in to master their skills and how hard they had to work to become what they are today.

Instead of putting in the work, most people tend to just set their goals and complain about the fact that they have not achieved them.

People want things now, they forget sometimes it takes years of hard work to even get an opportunity to achieve your goals.

This culture of lack of patience flows like blood in our societies and not just in youngsters.

Even though our generation has better resources and better opportunities at our disposal, it still takes time and patience to achieve the things we want, just like every other generation since the history of mankind.

The only thing that this impatience is good for is for causing anxiety.

 

Lack of Execution

You cant win the race that you didnt participate in.

 

As I mentioned before, we live in an information age where we can start learning anything due to a huge variety of online courses, blogs, webinars and eBooks like this one.

Because learning is so easy now, it seems illogical not to step up your knowledge by using all of these resources that are available to us.

So, we start learning about things we want, like how to start a blog, how to build a business, how to play guitar, how to sell stuff on eBay, how to monetize a YouTube channel.

However, the thing is that most of us, do everything, except for taking action.

Because there is unlimited content on the internet, the learning can go on forever.

Learning is highly beneficial, but unfortunately it has become a way for procrastination from actually doing and taking action.

Learning does seem a necessity to start something, but the thing is that, you will never be ready unless you start executing on your goals.

Mark Zuckerberg would have never built Facebook if he had not paused learning programming and used his existing skills to actually build his small project named the Facebook.

We will have to start learning by doing, not just by researching and consuming.

You will never find the kind of work you love doing unless you start doing it.

A big number of people never even start taking action towards their dreams and goals, and they are the ones who are the victims of anxiety.

 

False Positivity

Happiness has no value without sadness.

 

We live In a time where, things like reality television exists, and the even next level version of those are the vlogs on YouTube.

We like to watch these things because they give us an insight of the lives other than our own and through their perspective.

We get to see what other cultures look like and it really helps us get out of our own life and problems, and we get to see their problems through their eyes, even though it is just for a very short period of time.

It is the human nature of showcasing their better self and hiding away the shortcoming of their lives.

So, when a guy named Raj sees a video on the internet of a world-famous vlogger named Casey Neistat, he sees how much fun he is having in his life, how he can travel anywhere he wants, how he does anything he wants and can record his life while doing it.

This makes Raj think that his life is not nearly as good as Caseys and it probably sucks.

What he doesnt see is how much Casey had to work to get to that place where he can travel the world, how he manages his family and work while traveling and how his mood is when he is recording everything and then putting 4 hours a day to edit those videos.

This only makes Raj blindsided, he can only see what he is shown, which are only the positive side of Caseys life that he is showcasing, while editing out the non-fun parts.

This is not just the case of reality Tv, this also happens through the news.

Lets say, a person named Rahul has just launched his new startup and wants to be informed about all the latest updates that happen in the Indian startup ecosystem. So, he subscribes to the infamous blog of YourStory by liking their Facebook page.

One of the objectives YourStory is to inspire entrepreneurs by sharing the stories of upcoming successful entrepreneurs along with providing the news and the latest updates.

Every time, Rahul opens up Facebook he gets to see an article or two in his news feed of YourStory that says, how this entrepreneur raised 1Million$ in funding, or how that entrepreneur made 1Million$ in revenue.

What those articles doesnt say is how much stress the is on the guy who just raised someones money and now has to figure out how to grow his business, or how much of that revenue he made is actually profit.

Because, Rahul only sees the positive side of theose stories, it leads him to believe that everyone except him his achieving something and he is the only one who isnt successful. He thinks maybe thats because he is a terrible entrepreneur or he is just not as lucky as others.

All of these might just be misconceptions, but almost everyone can agree, that our mind never fails to create them, every once in a while.

This notion of false positivity is one of the major causes of dissatisfaction in our generation, and this is one of the reasons for our anxieties.

 

Expectations

Reality>Expectation. Secret to a satisfactory life.

 

It is perfectly natural for all Human beings to have expectations from their lives, careers, businesses, or even relationships. Some of us have expectations about how our lives are going be when we retire, or how our dream life partner would be like.

The thing about expectations is that they are very specific and are pretty rigid in nature, and it is just undeniably hard to let go of them.

Expectations are a great way to map someones mind. You can know what they care about, what they think about themselves and what are their abilities and capabilities, just asking this question, Where do you see yourself in 5 years?.

That is why so many job interviewers ask this question from the applicants because it tells them so much about the state of the mind of the applicant.

Expectations are mostly good because they help us to assign our goals and priorities in life, but it becomes a major problem when the reality falls short from those rigid expectations that we have set for ourselves.

Everyone have had expectations in their childhood of what they wanted to become when they grow up, and some expected of becoming an astronaut or a movie star, but when they grow up they realize how unrealistic those expectations actually were and how high they had set the bar for themselves, and they let go of it because those expectations were flexible to begin with.

And, then there are instances when we carefully and realistically set our expectations for ourselves, we set the bar that seems achievable for our future self. But, every once in a while, the reality fails to meet the expectations that we have set.

For example, when you expect to score an A grade in your exam but the result is B, it really hurts, and it hurts, even more, when you expect an A, and you get a C, the further away the reality it is from your expectation, the more it hurts. If only had you expected an F and gotten a C you would have been the happiest person in your school.

Expectations of grades are pretty common and don't really have any adverse long-term effect.

But when the expectations are set for something serious in life, something that really matters to a person, and the reality tends to fall short of those expectations, the result can be disastrous.

Say, a school student has a really strong expectation from himself that he will be able to get admission in one of the most prestigious universities in India, named the IITs.

The student prepares for it, works, hard, does everything he can to map his actions that can keep up with his ambitions.

But, even after all that effort he falls short, and is not able to crack his way into the IITs because of the intense competition.

The effect of that failure can leave an emotional scar on him, can lead him to believe that he cant achieve anything in life, that he has disappointed his parents and teachers.

In fact, this exact same burden of failure is what has led to so many student suicides in India, all because students have set such high expectations from themselves that when they realize they cannot be fulfilled, it leads them to a plunge of depression and some even take the next step of endinig their own life.

Not just in exams, these expectations are the root cause of dissatisfaction and unhappiness in all sorts ways.

A person who has unrealistic expectations from their spouse is always going to be unhappy or a person who wants to become a millionaire by the age of 25 and fails while always be disappointed in themselves.

Expectations can be an asset if used properly, but can be devastating if not.

 

Comparing Yourself to Others

Envy is the most petty feeling the world.

 

We live in a time when it is incredibly easy to show off your life using the different variety social media platforms. And it is in our human nature to do so.

We want to tell our friends that we jot a new job or that we are getting married. That is why social media is filled with such posts.

And it is also human nature, to hide the failures of our lives from our friends and acquaintances. That is why we dont see many negative posts from our friends online.

This could very well be considered the subject of false positivity, but the only reason why it affects some people is because they compare themselves to the lives of people who have nothing but success to show.

You wont see a post from your friend that says, I got divorced or that I lost my Job, all you see are is the news of people in your social circle who have achieved something in their lives.

And the people who fall into the trap of comparing themselves to others are the ones who end up most dissatisfied in life.

Sometimes they compare other peoples strengths with their weakness without realizing that everyone has their own unique strengths.

Lets say a friend of mine is an amazing artist and always showcases his paintings on social media if I compared my skill of painting I would always feel like a loser.

Sometimes, they even try to copy the successes of others without realizing that the path of everyones life is different.

The ones that do realize those facts are actually happy about the success of their peers and some even feel inspired by it.

This negative feeling of envy only makes us want we what we dont have, instead of appreciating the things that we already do have.

 

Taking Advice without context

A bad advice does more harm than a good advice does good.

 

In the early 20s, everyone knows that they have a lot to learn and it would not hurt to take advice from people that are more experienced.

Sometimes, it even helps us in figuring out the opportunies that we didnt even know existed.

Also, there is no shortage of people who are ready to give advice using their own experience. We have career consultants, relationship consultants, life consultants and consultants for pretty much for almost every other thing.

The one thing that these people dont have, is the context of people that they are consulting.

For example, if you are having fights with your girlfriend or boyfriend and you look for advice from your friends, they might advise you to break up or not based on their experience, without considering your love for your partner.

Same happens when you ask a more experienced person for career advice, they wont ask you what you want to do or what you are best at, instead, they will advise you based on their experiences.

Career counselors will advise you to peruse accounting or computer science if they dont know that you are passionate about photography.

And if you do end up taking that advice you may or may not be successful in life, but you will always regret not becoming a photographer.

Almost every successful artist has been advised to quit at least once in their lifetime.

It is always beneficial to learn from other peoples experience, but sometimes taking their advice can be harmful.

Like I mentioned before, everyone was advising Brian Chesky, the CEO of Airbnb to quit his startup, that it would never work. What they didnt know was how close Brian actually was from success.

Fortunately, Brian was aware of his business and what it would take to be successful, but if he had taken advice from people who didnt know that, he would have quit a long time ago and would have never became a billionaire.

There are thousands of successful people who have their own personal experiences and will most probably have their own advice to give.

But the only advice that is helpful and actionable is the one tailored for you.

 

Motive Driven Decisions

Good thing done with bad intent is just as bad as doing the bad thing.

 

For everything that we do in our lives, there is always a motive behind it.

Whether it is cleaning your clothes or buying a laptop or literally anything else you can think of.

But when it comes to long-term and serious decisions those motives have a real impact on our lives.

For example, there can be a million motivations behind starting a business, like you want to make money, or you want to be your own boss, or you want to solve a problem, or you want to help your community and countless others.

These motivations are drivers that keep us going, they push us to live upto our ambitions.

But when that motive is shallow and unfulfilling, we lose that drive to follow our ambitions, and it sometimes leads to massive failures and sometimes even quarter life crises.

Say, my motivation behind starting my new business is making money, that motivation an asset if what I am starting a hedge fund, I will be highly motivated and my business will also flourish because my motivation matches the driver of the business, which is making money.

But if I am starting a restaurant business, and my motivation behind is making money instead of serving great food and experience to the customers, I will always feel unfulfilled at my work, and my business will also suffer because of it. That is because my motivation is different from the driver of the business.

Same thing happens with actors, singers, directors and other artists who want to try to become a professional artist just for superficial things like fame and money instead of their love for the art.

They are always the ones who end up quitting, because they dont love what they do, and dont have that push to keep carrying on.

The real and successful artists are always the ones with pure motivation of the love for their art.

Similarly, there are young people in their early 20s who have already taken the decision of what they want to do in their life, and they have a superficial or shallow motivation behind it.

It can be anything, like working in a Nonprofit Organization to build your personal brand instead of actually supporting the cause for that Nonprofit Organization, or pursuing a profession for the money even if they dont know anything about it, or starting a YouTube show just for the fame and not for the actual creativity.

They are always the ones are prime victims of anxiety and often end up regretting the decision that they made.

Lack of focus

Take it one shot at a time.

 

Focus comes in rare supply in the 21st Century. We have so many apps on our phone, pushing out notifications every other minute and social media platforms who undeniably addicting to look at, combined with all our favorite songs, and videos just within our arms reach.

It is hard, not to be distracted in this information economy.

For everything that we want to achieve, we need focus to be productive, whether it be creating an app, preparing balance sheets or writing a book, it takes focus to execute.

This lack of focus often takes away our productivity, but it doesnt have much to do with anxiety.

But when this lack of focus is looked at through a macro lens, it does become concerning.

Distraction is not the notifications that pop up on your phones every minute.

The real distraction is the struggle of making a decision and sticking to it.

Because we have so much information in our hands, it gives us the ability to do so much, that couldnt have been done before; we can create music, we can make movies, we can build apps, websites and even build companies without even leaving the comfort our rooms.

It has become hard to choose one goal and focus on achieving it.

There are some of my friends who are doing so many things at once hoping that they will succeed at least one of them.

They are trying to peruse careers in finance, marketing, and Human Resources all at once.

Instead of choosing one profession and putting all their effort in one place, they are doing everything, just because they can.

This lack of focus is what keeps so many of us from greatness and success. And this the thing that causes us anxiety about what we are going to end up, within the future.

 

Sense of entitlement

No one deserves anything until they do.

 

Our Grandparents generation have gone through some of the toughest times in history. They have seen the times before Indian independence, they have witnessed world wars and have been through the great depression.

After being through all this chaos, they really understand the importance of stability in life. That is because it is human nature to value the things that we dont have.

Because of all that, they have taught their children the importance of stability in life, and how they should aim for it.

By learning from those lessons, our parents generation has significantly aimed for stability and have established a very strong foundation for us.

It includes financial stability, education, health care, housing and with all that a stable mindset. This always comes with their love and support in life.

And because of all that support and backing from our parents, our generation is one of the most risk-taking generation in modern history.

Our parents have distilled a confidence in our minds that we have the ability to achieve whatever we want in life.

And our risk-taking mind is ever ready to pursue the dreams that we have created for ourselves.

All of this confidence is programmed in our subconscious unknowingly by our parents.

Although, it might not be the case for everyone, but it is for the majority of our generation.

With all that love, support, confidence, sometimes the delusion of entitlement also enters into our subconscious.

We start to believe that we actually can achieve anything just because we want it more than everyone else, or just because we have worked hard for it, or sometimes just because we deserve it.

But in most cases that is not the case, in the fabric of reality, there are different levels of politics and outside factors that we cannot control.

I have witnessed several friends of mine who studied harder than anyone in all their college life and managed to achieve top GPAs on the campus, they were well prepared and highly confident that they would get a job in the large account firm, named EY. But when the recruiters came to our campus to hire some students, none of them were selected, instead, recruiters hired the ones with lower GPAs but better communication skills.

Although, this realization of the truth and the existence of their delusion of entitlement did open their minds, it also led them into a pit of anxiety about what are they going to do next.

 

The Past

We all are the prisoners of our past.

 

We live in a time where the past and the present exist on the same timeline. With the use of smartphones, cameras and other recorders, we can capture memories as an essence of time.

These memories can be saved in forms of videos, photographs, call recordings and text chats and the things like hard drives, and the internet lets us save those memories for life.

Also, it has never been easier to relive those memories just by accessing those recorded moments; it is like a time machine, and all we need is a screen to relive our past.

We can hold onto our past in a way that wasnt possible before.

 

It is the nature of our brains of recalling negative memories more often than the positive ones.

When we try to make an online payment, we dont remember the hundreds of times when that transaction successful, we recall the one time when we were defrauded. In fact, some people have just quit making online payments because of that fear.

It is not anyones fault, it could have happened to anybody, but the recall of those negative outcomes is just what comes naturally to our brains.

Every time we are about to do something big like trying out public speaking, we tend to get bombarded by the feelings of anxiety and nervousness. That is just our brain trying to recall the negative stuff trying to make us believe that something might go wrong.

The failures of our past hold us back both consciously and subconsciously.

This problem is persistent in many minds of 21-year-olds who are on the edge of the beginning of their new life, and their subconscious brain is making them anxious by recalling all the negative feelings that they have felt before. Those digitally stored memories are one of the reasons why our generation as a whole is facing this problem more than the previous ones.

 

The Solution

Each psychological problem is different and should be handled uniquely. There is no advantage in taking advice from anyone who doesnt really understand that problem.

Despite that, there is an array of solutions that might sound generic to some people but have actually solved the problem of anxiety for a huge number of youngsters.

Because Anxiety is the problem of the mind, it can only be solved from the inside.

These solutions have the capability of encountering and attacking various causes of anxiety.

The first step in solving any problem is admitting that the problem exists, second is the willingness to find the cause of that problem and then actually finding it.

Many people need help in finding it; some people are capable of finding its cause themselves.

The last step is always finding the solution for the problem and implementing it.

The solution can never work if the victim is not willing to implement it or doesnt admit that the problem exists in the first place.

But, the ones who have identified the cause of their anxieties and are in search for the solution, they always tend to find it in one of these.

 

Loved Ones

People closest to us care more about us than we realize.

 

The only people who help us in need of hour are the people closest to us who actually love us.

Sometimes, they are the ones, who actually point out that were the victims of anxiety in the first place.

They can be our parents, friends, close relatives or even our partners.

The thing that everyone needs to understand is that no one has actually figured out their life, even the most successful people face problems in their lives and need the help of their loved ones to get through them.

They not only provide support in any way possible, but they are also the ones who can actually help us find the unique solution to our problems since they know us better than anyone else.

Our loved ones can help us get out of any problem, they help us find our passion, they can give us support and the motivation to achieve any milestone that we want.

But, they can only help us if we let them, hiding your problems only leads to bigger problems. Asking for help always helps.

 

Patience

It takes time.

 

Be Patient, this does sound like the most generic advice one could ever give. It is also a popular one.

That is because it is the right advice. It is the thing that keeps us sane, and it is more important than most people realize.

Everyone have their own version of impatience, some cant wait to find their soul mate, some cant wait to become a lawyer, some cant wait to start their business, and some just cant wait to go to Spain. They all should have their own methods of overcoming their long-term or short-term impatience.

Overcoming impatience takes intense practicing and solid control over the mind. This might be easier said than done. But overcoming impatience is worth putting all of that effort.

The victims of impatience forget that life is about enjoying the journey, not about reaching the final destination that is death. They want to achieve their goals, they forget that the real fun is in the path of achieving that goal.

They forget that no successful person ever made it without putting in the time and the effort.

Being patient is not optional these days, because we have no other choice, as it only causes anxiety and is actually good for nothing.

Also, anything that is worth achieving in our lives is only worth it because of the process behind it, and every process requires patience.

It takes us time to figure out our lives, and luck also favors the person who is patient enough for it.

Patience can be a hard thing to achieve since it requires us to take control of our minds.

But one of the things that can help us to deploy patience is actually relaxing and enjoying the life we are living currently instead of anticipating the future.

Work is also one of the best solutions for overcoming impatience because it keeps us busy and keeps us heading towards our goal without giving us the time to measure the steps it would take to get there.

 

Focus

Pick one target and aim for the middle.

 

This sudden urge of doing everything at once has led us all to the path of failure at least once in our lives.

Just because we can do everything at once, doesnt mean we should.

Humans are not capable of multitasking efficiently.

This has even been proved by multiple types of research conducted throughout the world, yet we still continue to do it.

Whatever may be the reason or distraction behind it, it is necessary to address it.

We are much more likely to achieve success if we put all our efforts and focus on a single goal rather than pursuing multiple goals at once.

Lack of focus leads lack of determination and productivity for achieving any of the goals, and we tend up failing in all of them.

We have to force ourselves to pick up a single target and focusing on it until we achieve it.

Letting go of all the distractions from todays world might not be easy, but just like patience, this also needs practice and is always worth it.

 

Gratitude

It is a privilege to be able to afford this book. Be grateful.

 

The single biggest problem with our generation is the lack of gratitude.

We forget how good our lives actually are, compared to previous generations.

We can buy anything and get it delivered to us by a click of a button, we can contact and stay connected with anyone with even leaving the comfort of our homes.

Not just that we forget to appreciate little things how much our parents love us, how luxuriously we are living, or how we lavishly we get to eat.

We forget the fact that we were lucky enough to have all these things that most people in the world dont have.

Once we start acknowledging these details of our lives, we become more thankful for our lives.

This tiny switch of gratitude is easy to turn on all we need to do is start focusing on the positive aspects of our lives rather than complaining what we dont have.

Gratitude has the potential of making anyone truly grateful and happy for their lives and also has the ability to perish all problems such as anxiety, in an instant.

 

Be yourself

The only thing that separates us from the crowd is our identity.

 

In this dynamic world of influence, it is easy to forget who we are, what we really love, and what the things we are best at are.

To be actually successful and find fulfillment, we must find that out.

There are people who pursue things just because society wants them to, not because they want to, or just because a successful person has suggested them to.

What people forget is everyones path is different, and finding your own path is one of the major drivers of our lives.

We cannot just follow the footsteps of every successful person and expect to reach the same place they did. There are million other variables that have to be taken into account.

The journey of finding out our own passion and purpose is where the actual life happens. We can be wrong at times where we want to give in the advice of more experienced people, but what we have to realize is that everyone has been wrong at least once in their lives and sometimes and we have to stick to our ambitions and goals even if we are wrong. Because being wrong is better than losing your confidence and self-esteem.

Success is not a goal that can be achieved, people create milestones in their mind, and believe they can call themselves successful after they achieve it, it can be making a million dollars for someone and donating a million dollars for another.

Materialized success is never fulfilling. It cant even be defined in the type of work we do because there are only a handful of people who are able to find that one thing, that they are both passionate about and are best at it.

We all cannot become the best at what we do, we can only become the best version of ourselves.

Success is just a state of mind, that can be achieved in any position.

We can find both dissatisfied billionaires and happy homeless people.

Success is what fulfills us in life and truly makes us happy, which we have to find out for ourselves.

When we try to create our own way, we are curious to find out where it leads, but when we try to follow the path laid out by others, it makes us anxious to find whether it will lead us to the place that we want.

 

Just Start

Every Journey starts with a single step.

 

When we have to take an important decision, it is considered pretty common and useful to weigh the pros and cons of different options so we can speculate which one could be better.

And in the early 20s, choosing the right path for a career is one of those important decisions that we all have to make.

Some people try to find out their passion, the thing that we born to do, and in that process of finding that one thing, they start overanalyzing every option.

That over analyzation only makes the task of decision making harder.

The ones who do find their passion, dont realize that sometimes passion can be like a temporary boyfriend or girlfriend. You might feel like you are in love at the moment, but sometimes that thing doesnt last forever.

It is easier to lose passion than to find one.

The only thing that can prevent that anxiety of finding the perfect work is just starting something.

It is not possible to know if you like something or not without trying it first.

You cannot just say chocolate tastes terrible if you havent tried it before.

Instead of falling into the trap of overthinking and feeling jealous of other peoples achievements, the best way is to just start something and then decide later if you like it or not. There are a million other things that can be tried if it doesnt work.

Michael Jackson would have neither been a world class dancer nor an excellent singer if he had thought about picking that one thing that would make him successful instead of trying both of those things out.

Just planning and strategizing without the execution has never made anyone successful. It only makes people more anxious.

The only solution to this is closing your eyes, and taking that first step and finding out where it leads.

 

Reminder

Most of us go through this period of anxiety in our early 20s, and it can be a bit overwhelming at times. But it is not a liability unless it is extreme.

For the majority of us, Anxiety is an asset, it is the thing that drives us to stand up, get out of our comfort zones and actually take action to achieve the goals we have set and the dreams we have imagined.

Anxiety is the result of our ambitions; it is present only to remind us that we are not where we want to be.

It forces us to figure something out and doing something that actually gets us there.
